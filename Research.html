Research, Interests and Audit

Research

Fuelled by my undergraduate degree, one of the main areas of research I am interested in is regarding regenerative dentistry. One such example of this is a literature review: “Stem Cell Therapies to Recover Salivary Gland Function in Head and Neck Cancer Radiotherapy Patients”, published in the dental school monthly newspaper.

Souyave Prize in Endodontics

In my final year at dental school, I won the Souyave Prize in Endodontics for both my clinical work in endodontics, and a literature review: “Tissue Engineering in Regenerative Endodontics”.

Further to this, I am fascinated about the cellular and molecular mechanisms regarding osseointegration of implants.

I am also currently working towards a poster presentation on a patient introduced to me at a Multi-disciplinary meeting, regarding abnormal growth of a squamous cell carcinoma, covering the left dorsum of her tongue, however mapping biopsies show the SCC to only be a few cell layers deep. In producing this case, I will develop skills in public speaking and presentation as well as working within a multidisciplinary team, both of which, I believe will serve me well in this job role.




Clinical Interests

Whilst I enjoy all aspects of clinical dentistry, I am particularly drawn to Oral surgery and periodontology, hence I am keen to gain as much experience from my year in an oral surgery department to enhance my surgical skills as much as possible, with the hope of sitting Part 1 of the examinations for the Royal Colleges later this year.

SAAD Sedation Course

One surprise I learnt about myself working in practice was how much I enjoyed treating children and anxious patients, finding getting through a procedure alongside a particularly nervous patient, or a child extremely rewarding. However, I wanted to push myself further in being able to help these patients, and have signed up for the SAAD sedation course, which I will be attending in March 2020, further enhanced by the experience I am currently receiving in the Oral Surgery department.

Audit

Audits are vital in improving patient care, here are the audits I have been involved in:
  1. Adherence to Delivering Better Oral Health (DBOH) with regards to caries prevention – Malting’s Dental Practice

    Assessing compliance of clinicians on important but often overlooked aspects of caries prevention as stated in DBOH:
    • Prescription of high fluoride toothpaste for patients in active fixed appliance orthodontic treatment
    • Prescription of high fluoride toothpaste in high caries risk children ages 8-9
    • Assessment of brushing status of children up to 6 years old (e.g. supervised brushing, brushing alone etc)
    • Fluoride varnish application age limit (16 vs 18y.o.)

  2. Pre-operative Assessment of Conscious sedation patients – Queen Elizabeth Hospital

    3. Assessing compliance of assessing clinicians to department guidelines regarding suitability for conscious sedation. Currently in initial data collection phase.
